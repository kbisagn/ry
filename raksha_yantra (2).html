Background

History

In 1947, very few Indians had first-hand knowledge or experience of higher defence organisation. Pakistan-inspired invasion of Kashmir forced the pace of evolution. Several committees were formed — the Defence Committee of the Cabinet, the Defence Minister's Committee (DMC), the Chiefs of Staff Committee (COSC), the Joint Planning Committee (JPC), and the Joint Intelligence Committee (JIC).
In subsequent years, following ceasefire in Kashmir and India's adherence to a policy of peace and non-alignment, most committees became defunct, their functions combined into a streamlined structure.
After the Chinese aggression in 1962, the Defence Committee of the Cabinet was replaced by the Emergency Committee of the Cabinet. The DMC was revamped to include scrutiny of operational developments and overseeing defence preparedness — marking a turning point in India's integrated defence evolution.

Command Structure

Leadership

Raksha Mantri
Hon'ble RM
Minister of Defence, GOI
"

The Raksha Mantri is the principal civilian head of India's defence establishment, responsible for the overall policy direction, budgetary oversight, and inter-ministerial coordination of the Ministry of Defence. The Hon'ble Raksha Mantri chairs the Defence Acquisition Council (DAC) and provides strategic guidance to the three Services and allied organisations. Under this leadership, India has pursued a policy of Atmanirbhar Bharat — championing indigenous defence production, deepening strategic partnerships with allied nations, and modernising the armed forces to meet contemporary and future security challenges.

Hon'ble Raksha Mantri
Minister of Defence, Government of India
Chief of Defence Staff
General (CDS)
Principal Military Adviser
"

The Chief of Defence Staff (CDS) is the senior-most uniformed officer of the Indian Armed Forces and the single-point military adviser to the Government of India. The CDS heads the Department of Military Affairs (DMA) and is responsible for facilitating the restructuring of military commands for optimal utilisation of resources by bringing about jointness in operations, logistics, transport, training, support services, communications, repairs and maintenance. The CDS also oversees the tri-service organisations and ensures convergence in defence procurement and capability development.

General (CDS)
Chief of Defence Staff · Principal Military Adviser to GOI
CISC
Lt Gen (CISC)
Chairman, Chiefs of Staff
"

The Chief of Integrated Staff to Chairman Chiefs of Staff Committee (CISC) functions as the operational head of HQ Integrated Defence Staff. The CISC coordinates the activities of the three Services at the strategic and operational levels and is responsible for joint planning, doctrine development, and integrated resource management. As Chairman of the Chiefs of Staff Committee Secretariat, the CISC ensures that tri-service coordination and joint capability development receive the highest level of attention, driving the vision of a truly integrated and joint fighting force for India.

Lt Gen (CISC)
Chief of Integrated Staff to Chairman, COSC
DCIDS
Maj Gen (PP&FD)
Plans, Policy & Force Dev.
"

The Deputy Chief of Integrated Staff (Plans, Policy & Force Development) — DCIDS (PP&FD) — is responsible for long-term strategic planning, policy formulation, and the integrated force development roadmap across the Indian Armed Forces. This role entails oversight of joint doctrine, theatre command conceptualisation, and force structure reviews. DCIDS (PP&FD) provides the analytical underpinning for capability-based planning, ensuring that procurement priorities, manpower policies, and operational concepts are aligned with India's evolving national security imperatives.
